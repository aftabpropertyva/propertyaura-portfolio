Making Tax Digital 2026: The Complete Landlord Guide

Making Tax Digital (MTD) for Income Tax is the biggest change to the UK tax system in decades. From April 2026, millions of landlords will be required to keep digital records and provide quarterly updates to HMRC.

Who is affected?

From 6 April 2026, MTD applies to self-employed individuals and landlords with a total qualifying income of more than £50,000. From April 2027, this threshold drops to £30,000.

The New Requirements

  • Digital Record Keeping: You must use HMRC-compatible software to record all business income and expenses.
  • Quarterly Updates: Instead of one annual tax return, you'll send four quarterly summaries of your income and expenses.
  • Final Declaration: A final end-of-period statement to confirm your total income and claim any reliefs.

Key Deadlines

Quarterly updates are due within one month of the end of each quarter:

  • Quarter 1 (6 Apr - 5 Jul): Due 5 Aug
  • Quarter 2 (6 Jul - 5 Oct): Due 5 Nov
  • Quarter 3 (6 Oct - 5 Jan): Due 5 Feb
  • Quarter 4 (6 Jan - 5 Apr): Due 5 May

How to Prepare

  1. Check your income: Use our MTD Calculator to see if you meet the threshold.
  2. Choose Software: Start looking for HMRC-compatible software now. Don't wait until 2026.
  3. Separate Finances: If you haven't already, open a separate bank account for your property business.
