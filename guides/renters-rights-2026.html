Renters' Rights Act 2026: 5 Things to Do Before 31 May

The Renters' Rights Act 2026 represents the most significant overhaul of the private rented sector in over 30 years. Here are the five critical steps every landlord must take before the 31 May deadline.

1. Understand the Abolition of Section 21

The Act removes the ability for landlords to evict tenants without a reason (no-fault evictions). You will now need to provide a valid ground for possession, such as wanting to sell the property or move back in yourself.

2. Review Your Rent Increase Process

Rent increases will be limited to once per year and must be in line with market rates. The new process requires a formal notice period and gives tenants the right to challenge increases via a tribunal.

3. Meet the Decent Homes Standard

For the first time, the Decent Homes Standard will apply to the private rented sector. Ensure your property is free from serious hazards and meets modern standards for kitchens, bathrooms, and heating.

4. Prepare for the New Ombudsman

All private landlords will be required to join a new government-approved Ombudsman scheme. This provides a quicker and cheaper way for tenants to resolve disputes without going to court.

5. Update Your Documentation

Ensure all your tenancy agreements and compliance documents are up to date. Use our Renters' Rights Act Compliance Kit to make sure you don't miss any critical requirements.
